Top Movers: Vernova Surge, Gold Softness, Travel Pullback

GE Vernova (GEV) emerged as the top performer this week, rallying +6.66% and reversing recent losses. This move highlights ongoing strength in the industrial and renewables space, suggesting continued investor interest in energy transition plays.

Gold miner Newmont Corp (NEM) saw mild softness, dipping -2.46% for the week despite recent resilience. This slight pullback comes amid broader risk-off sentiment but NEM remains positive at +2.7% total return since inception.

Travel and leisure stock Royal Caribbean Cruises (RCL) declined sharply by -3.55%, deepening its drawdown to -16.61% total. The cruise sector continues to face macro headwinds and profit-taking after a strong summer run.

Tech hardware names Western Digital (WDC) and Seagate Technology (STX) both held up relatively well, with WDC down -2.03% (still +28.63% total) and STX off -1.3% (+16.89% total). These positions remain core drivers of portfolio outperformance.

Palantir Technologies (PLTR) and CVS Health (CVS) underperformed, falling -3.9% and -3.54% respectively this week. Both stocks are in minor drawdown since inception but remain strategic AI and healthcare holdings.
